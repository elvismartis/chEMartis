About Me

Elvis is currently a post-doctoral fellow at US2B, Nantes University. Previously, he was an Assistant Professor of Pharmaceutical Chemistry at Bombay College of Pharmacy and a computational chemist working on various drug discovery projects.

After earning a bachelor’s degree in Pharmacy from V.E.S. College of Pharmacy (University of Mumbai) in 2011, Elvis pursued a master’s degree in Pharmaceutical Chemistry at Bombay College of Pharmacy in 2014. He completed his PhD in Computational Chemistry under Prof. Dr. Evans Coutinho in 2019.

During his PhD, Elvis developed methods to understand drug-resistant mutations using computer simulations. His current research is focused on molecular modelling techniques to understand chiral separation using cyclodextrins, protein-ligand interactions, and QSAR modelling.

You can find my publications on Google Scholar.

Elvis also serves as an editorial board member and reviewer for various journals in computational chemistry and bioinformatics.

Feel free to contact me if you need more information.
